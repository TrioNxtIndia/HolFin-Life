Medicare Advantage (Part C) and Medicare Supplement plans offer coverage for expenses not covered by Original Medicare (Parts A and B). It's important to note that these two types of coverage are distinct, and individuals cannot enroll in both simultaneously.

Estate Planning and Legacy (Will & Trust)

Estate planning is the essential process of organizing the management and transfer of assets after death or in case of incapacity. It includes authorizing a Power of Attorney to make financial, property, or medical decisions on your behalf.

  • A Power of Attorney grants authority to an individual (the attorney-in-fact) to make financial, property, or medical decisions on your behalf.
  • A Healthcare Surrogate Designation, or Health Care Power of Attorney, allows you to appoint a person to make healthcare decisions on your behalf if you become incapacitated.
